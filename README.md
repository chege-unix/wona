Wona
Wona means "to see" in Chichewa and Nyanja, spoken across Malawi, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.
Wona is an open-source assistive vision system for blind and low-vision users in Africa. It runs on affordable consumer smart glasses — starting with Meta Ray-Ban — and delivers real-time audio descriptions of the surrounding environment, so users can hear what they cannot see.

Why Wona?
Over 12 million people in Sub-Saharan Africa live with blindness or severe visual impairment. Most existing assistive tools are too expensive, require custom hardware, or are designed for Western contexts — unfamiliar roads, currencies, languages, and environments.
Wona is built differently. It is Africa-first, trained and tested on African environments, signage, currencies, and local languages. It piggybacks on hardware people can already access, with no custom device required. It is designed to work offline and on low-bandwidth connections common across the continent. And it is completely free and open source.

What It Does
Wona listens through the camera on your glasses and speaks back to you. Point your gaze at the world around you and Wona will tell you what is there — obstacles ahead, text on a sign, the denomination of a banknote, a face you recognise, the name of a place.
Current development targets:

Real-time obstacle and hazard detection with directional audio cues
Currency recognition for East, West, and Southern African currencies
Text and signage reading — storefronts, transport, menus, labels
African language support including Swahili, Hausa, Zulu, Amharic, Chichewa, and Yoruba
On-device inference so core features work without internet access
Landmark and location awareness for navigation


Hardware
Wona is designed to run on  Smart Glasses paired with an Android companion phone.The goal is always to use hardware that is accessible and affordable — no custom devices, no expensive proprietary kit.
